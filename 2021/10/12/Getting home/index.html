Getting Home
Date: 11/11/2019, Photographer: Miss S, Post-Processing: Science Cookie

This photograph reminds me the Chinese idiom: Getting Home.

在中文里是一个很美的词，落叶归根。

There is a story hidden in this idiom. Historically chinese people tend to move around to take up better job positions. But when we retire, we feel an intrinsic need to go back to our hometown. That is the chinese “getting home” term accepted and expected within the society.

Personally, I have been moving around for education since my primary school. I stayed in different cities and sometimes even different countries for years, before heading over to a new place. For a long long time I haven’t felt at home anywhere, as I never have enough time to acclimatise and ingratiate myself within the city. I have never walked down the same road every day, never said hi to the same neighbours every morning and never lay on the same bed every night for decades together. However, I found comfort in the statement, “Hometown is where your heart is” by the German influencer Thomas Derksen, who has had a similar experience. Now, as a global citizen, my definition of “Getting Home” is to go somewhere that makes me happy.

See ya 😘
