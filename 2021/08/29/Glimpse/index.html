Glimpse
Date: 22/06/2021, Photographer: Miss S, Post-Processing: LxG and little青葱

This photo was taken on my way home from an exhibition at the University of Greenwich. We caught a glimpse of this breathtaking piece of ancient architecture. As a poor student, the 13.5 pounds entry fee was a bit too pricey to explore. Upon further research, I found that the Cutty Sark was a premium tea clipper built in 1896 by Scott & Linton. The once fast-moving steamship stands as the last tea clipper in the world1,2. A relic of the past that still flaunts its sails in pride as a symbol of the history and culture of the land. The picture below captures Cutty Sark in its glory days3.
