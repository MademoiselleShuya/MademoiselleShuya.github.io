Exhale
Date: 01/10/2021, Photographer: Miss S, Post-Processing: Science Cookie

Exhale is a Bionic Chandelier designed by Julian Melchiorri[1], taking inspiration from nature and the V&A’s Art-Nouveau and Islamic Art collections. As an artifact, its function is two-fold. Exhale serves as an artistic decoration while purifying the air indoors through photosynthesis performed by living photosynthetic microorganisms enclosed into leaf modules. This blend of art and science fascinates me.

Exhale is now part of the prestigious permanent collection at V&A, London. A textbook illustration of how advanced biomimetic engineering can be applied to improve the quality of our lives through everyday objects and architecture [2]. In fact, Exhale isn’t Melchiorri’s first piece exploring the immiscibility of art and science. His earliest work dates back to the Silk Leaf, 2014[3].

I keep telling myself I am done with research, and I’m going to the industry once I receive my Ph.D. degree. Yet, when I first came across this awe-inspiring masterpiece, it roused my latent interest in science. I came to the realization that I found experiments boring because I failed to create something novel.

Suddenly I felt I understood Ngenie better. He said, “I want to start my business because I want to produce my work, not to just become rich financially”. This sense of fulfillment makes people happy. If you find something you fancy, you nurse it with care and love, feed it with sweat, tears, and maybe even blood… funnily enough, it eventually defines who you are.

In the search to make it complete, it makes you complete!
